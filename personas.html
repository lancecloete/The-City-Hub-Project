Solutions
Emily Wong

Wicket Problem: Lack of access to fresh, organic produce in Windhoek's supermarkets, especially during the off-season.

Current Approach: Many residents rely on imported produce, which can be more expensive and less fresh. Some supermarkets have started to source locally, but availability is limited.

Potential Solution: The Superplatform could facilitate partnerships between local farmers and supermarkets to increase the supply of fresh, organic produce, providing infrastructure, training, and marketing support to farmers.

Ahmed Hassan

Wicket Problem: Difficulty in finding consistent quality and variety of products at open markets in Windhoek, especially for businesses that require large quantities.

Current Approach: Many businesses rely on a mix of open markets and supermarkets to source their supplies, which can be time-consuming and challenging.

Potential Solution: The Superplatform could create a centralized platform for businesses to connect with local producers and suppliers, offering features like online ordering, quality assurance, and bulk discounts.

David Kim

Wicket Problem: Difficulty in finding reliable and affordable cleaning services in Windhoek, especially for large properties like apartment complexes.

Current Approach: Many property managers rely on word-of-mouth recommendations or online directories to find cleaning services, which can be time-consuming and risky.

Potential Solution: The Superplatform could connect property managers with verified cleaning service providers, offering features like online booking, reviews, and quality assurance.

John Lee

Wicket Problem: Limited access to high-speed internet in certain areas of Windhoek, especially for businesses and remote workers.

Current Approach: Many residents rely on mobile data or slower internet connections, which can hinder productivity and connectivity.

Potential Solution: The Superplatform could collaborate with telecommunications providers to expand internet infrastructure and offer affordable, high-speed internet packages to residents and businesses.

Daniel Green

Wicket Problem: Limited access to specialized healthcare services in Windhoek, especially for chronic conditions or complex treatments.

Current Approach: Many residents rely on referrals to South African hospitals or travel abroad for specialized care, which can be expensive and time-consuming.

Potential Solution: The Superplatform could facilitate partnerships between local healthcare providers and international specialists, offering telemedicine services, medical tourism packages, and access to specialized equipment.
